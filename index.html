Temperature Range

The Vivo Chamber is capable of providing a stable operating temperature up to 90°C, meeting the requirements for the use of various specifications of capillary columns in the current market.

Heating Rate and Temperature Homogeneity

At room temperature, the heating rate can reach 2°C per minute, and approximately 60°C can be achieved within 15 minutes. Precise temperature calibration is completed before reaching the preset temperature, enabling precise control over the preset temperature.

The Vivo Chamber features an aluminum alloy frame design, which ensures rapid and stable heat conduction. The chamber is equipped with multiple heating modules built into the interior, providing a uniform thermal distribution and ensuring the consistency of the heating effect. The temperature sensing utilizes high-precision platinum resistance thermometers from Germany, known for their quick response and high accuracy. This ensures that the temperature is evenly stable at different positions within the Vivo Chamber.

Stability

The design of the low-voltage output power supply ensures the safety of use. The high-precision temperature controller, through microcomputer control, operates multiple high-power heating modules within the Vivo Chamber, ensuring that the operating temperature of the Vivo Chamber chamber fluctuates within ±0.1°C. In the long-term continuous operation test lasting up to 2 years, the entire device meets the requirements for stability and precision.

Safety

The AccuTemp Box is equipped with over-temperature alarm and protection functions. When the temperature exceeds the preset safety threshold, it will automatically trigger an alarm and cut off the heating output to prevent overheating. It also has a self-diagnostic function that can detect and display system faults, making it easy to identify and resolve issues promptly. The device's all-aluminum casing is designed with a certain level of water and dust resistance, protecting the internal circuits from environmental damage. The Vivo Chamber features an all-aluminum alloy frame design, with built-in heating modules made using ceramic heating technology, which have a dry burn function and do not generate electromagnetic interference during the heating process, making it suitable for use in environments that require electromagnetic compatibility.
